Restaurant Customer Behaviour & Tip Prediction Analysis

Project Overview

This project analyzes customer tipping behaviour in a restaurant environment and develops predictive models to estimate tip amounts using Excel-based analytics.

The objective is to identify factors influencing customer tips, understand customer spending behaviour, evaluate business performance, and provide actionable recommendations to restaurant management.

---

Business Problem

Restaurant management wanted to understand:

* What factors influence tipping behaviour?
* Which customer segments generate the highest revenue?
* Which days and time periods are most profitable?
* Can customer tips be predicted using historical data?
* How can staffing and customer service be optimized?

---

Dataset

The dataset contains 243 restaurant transactions with the following variables:

* Sex
* Smoker
* Day
* Time
* Size
* Total Bill
* Tip

Target Variable:

* Tip Amount

Key Findings

* Total Bill is the strongest predictor of Tip.
* Dinner customers leave higher tips than lunch customers.
* Saturday generates the highest revenue.
* Group Size positively influences tips.
* Model 2 (Total Bill + Size) provides the best balance between accuracy and simplicity.

---

Model Performance

Best Model: Model 2

* Adjusted R² = 0.462
* RMSE ≈ 1.01

This indicates that Total Bill and Group Size explain a significant portion of customer tipping behaviour.

---

Business Recommendations

* Allocate experienced staff during dinner hours.
* Increase staffing during weekends, especially Saturday.
* Target medium and high-value customer segments.
* Collect additional customer satisfaction variables to improve prediction accuracy.
